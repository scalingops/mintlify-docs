<Info>
**Architecture reference: proactive data flow.** This map shows the **proactive** side of MSPortal — how insights from integrations and assessments become Goals, Planner items, Budgets, Meetings and Reports. Reactive and operational data (PSA tickets and projects) flow to reporting through a separate pipeline, shown on the right side of the diagram.
</Info>

### Legend

| Marker | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Gray block with plug icon | **Integration** — external system MSPortal syncs from (PSA, RMM, M365, Backup &amp; Security) |
| White block | **MSPortal module** — a native feature built on top of the integration data |
| Dashed amber block | **External / reactive** — PSA-sourced projects and tickets that flow straight to reporting |
| Solid teal arrow | **Primary flow** — the main path a record takes through the pipeline |
| Gray arrow | **Data feed** — supporting data that enriches a downstream module |
| Dashed amber arrow | **External pipeline** — reactive PSA data routed to reports and MSP Tools |
| Dashed teal arrow | **Writeback to integration** — a record MSPortal creates or updates in the source system (for example, a PSA project created from the Onboarding Portal) |
| Teal circle badge | Item appears in **Reports &amp; Dashboards** |
| Amber circle badge | Item can be added to a **Meeting** agenda |

## Reading the diagram

The diagram is laid out in stages, left-to-right on integrations and top-to-bottom on flow.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Onboarding" icon="rocket">
    Kickoff intake and client setup. The Onboarding Portal writes a project back to your PSA and pulls survey responses in for the first engagement.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stage 0 — Integrations" icon="plug">
    M365, RMM, Backup &amp; Security, and PSA connect once and continuously feed the rest of the pipeline. The PSA block spans both the proactive side (Tool Stack reconciliation) and the reactive side (Projects and Tickets).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stage 1 — Collect" icon="inbox">
    Five input sources land data in MSPortal: **Surveys**, **Microsoft 365**, **Backup &amp; Security**, **Devices**, and **Tool Stack**. Each is its own module with its own views and filters.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stage 2 — Refine" icon="filter">
    **Goals** capture what the client wants to achieve. **Compliance** scores controls against frameworks. Both feed the Planner.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stage 3 — Plan" icon="list-check">
    The **Planner** is the center of the system. Every item carries title, status, priority, start/due dates, estimated hours and cost, linked devices, linked source, assignee, and company.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stage 4 — Budget" icon="calculator">
    **Budgets** roll labor up from planner hours and roll projects and products up from planner cost estimates and PSA items. A **Forecast** widget projects the budget forward.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stage 5 — Present" icon="presentation-screen">
    **Meetings** are the live client-facing output. **Reports &amp; Dashboards** is the single reporting surface — three formats: block-based reports, dashboards, and narrative reports.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Reactive rail" icon="ticket">
    On the right, **Projects** and **Tickets** flow from PSA directly into Reports &amp; Dashboards without passing through Planner. **Ticket Trends** and **Financial Reporting** roll up into **Client Health** for a portfolio view.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What lives on the Planner

Every planner item carries the same core attributes, no matter where it came from:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Title & status" icon="tag">Name, status, priority.</Card>
  <Card title="Dates & hours" icon="calendar">Start date, due date, estimated hours.</Card>
  <Card title="Cost estimate" icon="dollar-sign">Dollar cost used by Budgets.</Card>
  <Card title="Linked devices" icon="laptop">Which machines this work affects.</Card>
  <Card title="Linked source" icon="link">Goal, compliance control, tool, or survey that originated the item.</Card>
  <Card title="Assignee & company" icon="user">Who owns it, which client.</Card>
</CardGroup>

## The unified reporting surface

Reports &amp; Dashboards is one surface that pulls from the full proactive pipeline **and** from reactive PSA data. It offers three formats:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Block-based reports" icon="grid-2">
    Widget grid. Build once as a template, reuse across clients.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dashboards" icon="chart-line">
    Live metrics, real-time views. Drop them into the meeting or the client portal.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Narrative reports" icon="file-lines">
    Long-form QBRs and executive summaries. Claude generates the narrative from the same data.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Writebacks: MSPortal updates the source system

A handful of flows write **back** to the integration they came from. These appear as dashed teal arrows in the diagram.

- **Onboarding Portal → PSA.** When a client completes the kickoff intake, MSPortal creates a project in your PSA so the onboarding work lives where your team already operates.
- **Devices → PSA.** Warranty dates, end-of-life status, and other device fields sync back to PSA assets so your techs see the same numbers on a ticket as your AM sees in Budgets.

## The reactive rail

The right-hand side of the diagram is a separate pipeline. PSA-sourced **Projects** and **Tickets** do not pass through Planner. They land directly in Reports &amp; Dashboards, and they aggregate into two MSP-wide views:

- **Ticket Trends** — weekly, per-company, and per-category analytics.
- **Financial Reporting** — revenue efficiency, seat pricing, support cost.

Both roll up into **Client Health**, the portfolio view across every client in your book.

## The typical client journey

The same pipeline runs quarter after quarter.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="1. Kickoff" icon="flag">
    Onboarding survey. Connect M365. Import devices from the RMM. First compliance assessment.
  </Card>
  <Card title="2. Analyze" icon="magnifying-glass-chart">
    Turn survey responses into goals. Review compliance failures and Secure Score recommendations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="3. Plan" icon="list-check">
    Create planner items from goals, compliance findings, and tool recommendations. Attach devices. Set costs and hours.
  </Card>
  <Card title="4. Budget" icon="calculator">
    Roll planner costs into line items. Forecast forward. Share with the client for approval.
  </Card>
  <Card title="5. Present" icon="presentation-screen">
    Build a QBR agenda with goals, planner, compliance, and budgets. Present live, then send a report.
  </Card>
  <Card title="6. Repeat" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Follow-up survey. Planner items close out. New goals open. Next quarter starts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
